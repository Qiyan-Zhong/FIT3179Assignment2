Football stadium in Europe

With the hundreds years' development, nowadays, football has became a traditional and essential sport in modern society. Some countries have orgnized professional leagues to provide professional matches between clubs, especially in Europe, hundreds of clubs can be seen in most of European countries. Each Club has an independent stadium.

The maps on the right side display the stadiums which belong to clubs in highest level league in England, Scottland, France, Germany, Spain.

The stadium distribution is relatively cluster in England, it is due to there are more than one club in one city such as London, Chelsea, Arsenal, Tottenham Hotspur, etc are all stand in London. The stadium distribution are relatively seperate in France and Spain

The largest capacity of stadium in Europe is 'Camp Nou' which belongs to team Barcelona, Spain. 'Santiago Bernabeu' stadium with team Ream Madrid has the second largest capacity in Europe.

Football rank change trend (2008-2018

The chart display on the left side displays the rank change trend among five European countries, Englandm, France, Germany, Italy, Spain from 2008 to 2018.

Italy has the highest rank in 2008, but its' rank was continuously drop and Italy became the lowest rank among five countries in 2018.

Spain became the first rank in 2008, and Spain keeps it stable from 2008 to 2014. However, Spain's rank became unstable goes and down from June, 2014.

Geramny's rank is relatively stable among these counties, in most of time during this decade, the rank of Germany is equal or less than 5 which shows Germany's football strength and stable performance.

England and France's rank are not stable during this period. The difference between two rank publish date sometimes is larger rather than other three countries. France reach the lowest rank which is 27 in September, 2010

Football Transfer fee trend from 2000 to 2019

There are two transfer windows in summer and winter for clubs to buy, loan or sell players each year.With the development of society and professional football. The worth of professional football players is growing.

The chart displays in the right side shows the trend of total fee for top 250 valuable deals between clubs from 2000 to 2019.

In 2000, the number is around 2 billions dollars, however, the number drops from season '2002-2003', and the number reach the smallest which is about 1.1 billions dollars. From season '2004-2005', this number continuously grew and reach the largest point in season '2017-2018' which is about 4.7 billions dollars.
